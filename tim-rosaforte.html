Tim began his career at the Tampa Times in 1977 and has since worked for the Sun-Sentinel from 1981 to 1987, The Palm Beach Post from 1987 to 1993, and Sports Illustrated from 1994 to 1996. He then joined the Golf Digest / Golf World team, where he has worked since 1996. Since 2007, he has also worked as a contributor for the Golf Channel and as a commentator for NBC during the PGA Tour. He is one of a small number of journalists who can operate in both television and print at the same time. Rosaforte was president of the Golf Writers Association of America.

Tim has written five best-selling books: The PGA Tour (1990), Heartbreak Hill: Anatomy of a Ryder Cup (1996), Tiger Woods: The Making of a Champion (1997), Raising the Bar: Tiger Woods’ Championship Years (2000), and the 1998 World Golf Hall of Fame Yearbook, which he co-wrote with Jaime Diaz.

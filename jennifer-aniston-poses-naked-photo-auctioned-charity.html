Jennifer Aniston poses naked and Jennifer Lopez braves big cats for incredible charity auction pics

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Thursday, April 4, 2024

JENNIFER Aniston is supporting the auction of a nude black-and-white photo of herself in a bid to raise vital funds for a coronavirus relief charity.

The Friends actress, 51, along with Jennifer Lopez, backed her photographer pal Mark Seliger's decision to auction off the naked portrait on Instagram this week.

In the artistic shot, which was taken at the peak of Friends' popularity in 1995, the Along Came Polly star posed with her legs crossed in front of her as she stared down the camera lens.

Resting her head on her hands, Jennifer's signature hairstyle at the time, the 'Rachel,' named after her character on the sitcom, framed her facial features with aplomb.

Promoting the charity auction on her Instagram, the SAG winner wrote: "My dear friend @markseliger teamed up with @radvocacy and @christiesinc to auction 25 of his portraits - including mine - for COVID-19 relief...

"100% of sales proceeds of this portrait will go to @NAFClinics, an organization which provides free coronavirus testing and care nationwide to the medically underserved."

"Link in my bio to learn more about the auction. Thank you again to Mark for allowing me to be part of this #radart4aid."

Sharing the portrait, The Morning Show actress also posted an image of Mark working in his photography studio.

Jennifer isn't the only star to have their portraits auctioned off for charity as other shots also include Leonardo DiCaprio, Dr Dre, Snoop Dogg and Billie Eilish to name a few.

Notably a Jennifer Lopez 2001 portrait by Seliger features in the auction.

The snap sees the Hustlers star posing in between two leopards while clad in a bikini that appears to emulate armour.

The 50-year-old's long brunette hair falls down her waist as she poses on rugs and a circular shield stands behind her.

Meanwhile, actress Julia Louis-Dreyfus followed Jennifer Aniston's lead in 2014 and posed nude in another of Seliger's iconic shots.

In the shot, the Seinfield star, 59, peers over her shoulder showcasing a tattoo of the U.S. Constitution signed by John Hancock across her back.

However at the time, the picture caused waves when the public pointed out that Hancock signed the Declaration of Independence, not the Constitution.

Acclaimed photographer Mark Seliger's portraits of politicians, musicians, actors and celebrities have featured on the covers of Rolling Stone, GQ and Vanity Fair.

The limited-edition prints were being sold through a special philanthropic auction at Christie’s, with the goal of raising as much money as possible for charity during these unprecedented times from May 28.

Meanwhile, Jennifer's show of support for the charity auction comes a month after she took to Instagram to share a throwback image of herself sticking her middle finger up to the coronavirus.

She made her feelings towards the pandemic clear, as she captioned the image: "Dear Covid...⁣ You can kindly F@!k off now thank you BYE."

The Friends star got plenty of celebrity love for the post, with her Friends co-star Lisa Kudrow applauding the post.

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Friday, May 3, 2024

Jon Rahm is a celebrated Spanish professional golfer who achieved the world no. 1 spot in the Official World Golf Ranking in July 2020. Rahm is the second golf player from Spain to attain this position after Seve Ballesteros. 

Moreover, Jon Rahm is the fastest Spanish golfer in history to have five European Tour title wins within five years of their professional career.

The 2021 US Open Champion has created a name for himself as one of the finest golf players in the world. He became the first amateur golfer to receive the Ben Hogan Award for two consecutive years in 2015 and 2016.

During the beginning of his professional career, Rahm was infamous for being hot-headed as he often lost his temper on the golf course. However, he believed he performed his best when his frustration reached a breaking point. 

Regardless of his fiery personality, we cannot deny that the 2019 Race to Dubai Champion is one of the most compelling golfers in the last decade.

Jon Rahm Bio: Early Life, Family, and Education

Jon Rahm Rodríguez was born on November 10, 1994, in Barrika, Biscay, Spain. Edorta Rahm, his father, was from Basque Country, while his mother, Ángela Rodríguez, belonged to Madrid.

Rahm was interested in sports from a very young age, and he started playing golf before the age of 7. Similarly, he participated in canoeing, jai alai, soccer, and kung fu until 14. After that, he left these sports activities to focus on a golf career.

Hence, he practiced golfing at Centro Nacional de Golf, the model course in Madrid created by Spain’s Royal Golf Federation. 

His parents encouraged him to play golf since they enjoyed the sport themselves. Edorta and Ángela first took up golf after seeing the 1997 Ryder Cup in Valderrama, so they naturally taught the sport to their children. 

Jon attended IES Ortega y Gassett, a high school in Madrid, Spain. He won several European golf competitions, like the Jacques Léglise Trophy (2011) and Bonallack Trophy (2012). 

His father persuaded him to apply for college in the United States because of better career opportunities. So in 2012, he went to Arizona State University on a golf scholarship after being recruited by golf coach Tim Mickelson.

He graduated from Arizona State with a Bachelor of Arts degree in Communications.

Since Rahm grew up in Spain, he learned English phrases by listening to hip-hop music. His favorite songs include Love the Way You Lie by Eminem and Swimming Pools by Kendrick Lamar. 

John Rahm: Career

Now, let us take a look at Rahm’s career through the years.

Collegiate Career

Jon Rahm was considered one of the best college golf players, and he received many awards and honors during his amateur years.

During his junior year of college, he participated in the 2015 Phoenix Open as an amateur and finished fifth place. In April 2015, he achieved the no. 1 rank in the World Amateur Golf Ranking. 

He was honored with the 2014 Eisenhower Trophy as the World Amateur Team Championship medalist. Likewise, he received the 2015 and 2016 Ben Hogan Award for his outstanding performance as a college golfer. 

He was presented the Mark H. McCormack Medal for being the leading player in the World Amateur Golf Ranking in 2015. In addition, he won the Pac-12 Conference championship and an NCAA regional championship before the end of his collegiate career.

In short, Rahm has won 11 college golf tournaments, and he was ranked no. 1 in the World Amateur Golf Ranking for 60 record-breaking weeks. 

Rookie Years: 2016-2017

Rahm turned professional golfer after the 2016 US Open, and he played his first pro golf event at the Quicken Loans National. 

He won the Farmers Insurance Open in January 2017, and it helped his ranking move up from 137th to 46th in the Official World Golf Ranking. Moreover, it was his first PGA Tour win since the start of his professional career.

Rahm competed in the World Golf Championships event for the first time on March 2, 2017, at the WGC-Mexico Championship. He became the runner-up in the finals but reached the #14 ranking. 

During his first PGA European Tour event, he tied for 10th place in the Open de France. After that, he achieved his first European Tour win the Dubai Duty-Free Irish Open by six strokes.

Rahm also secured a win in the DP World Tour Championship, Dubai, the final event of the 2017 European Tour, by one stroke. As a result, he was named the European Tour Rookie of the Year.

Professional Career: 2018-2021

In 2018, he won the Desert Classic tournament, the second PGA Tour victory of his career. Moreover, he reached the no. 2 ranking in the Official World Golf Ranking.

Rahm won the Open de España in April, and he was qualified to be a part of the European team for the 2018 Ryder Cup. After defeating the United States team, the European team won the Cup with 17.5 to 10.5 at Le Golf National, France. 

In April 2019, Rahm and his partner Ryan Palmer won the Zurich Classic of New Orleans on the PGA Tour. In July, he won the Dubai Duty-Free Irish Open again by two strokes. Similarly, his second Open de España win happened in October. 

Jon Rahm received the title of European Tour Golfer of the Year after winning the 2019 European Tour with his final victory at the DP World Tour Championship. 

Rahm reached the no. 1 spot in the Official World Golf Ranking thanks to his Memorial Tournament win in July 2020. 

In June 2021, he could not compete in the Memorial Tournament since he tested positive for COVID-19. However, he won his first US Open tournament after finishing two birdies on the final two holes. 

Rahm received the PGA Player of the Year award for his noted 2020-21 season performance. Similarly, he won the Vardon Trophy and the Byron Nelson Award as the leader in scoring average. 

As of 2021, Jon Rahm has 13 professional wins (6 PGA Tour and 7 European Tour) with no. 1 ranking worldwide for 21 weeks.

Jon Rahm Bio: Marriage and Kids

Jon Rahm and Kelley Cahill met each other at Arizona State University. Cahill was a javelin thrower in college, so the two student-athletes bonded over their shared interest in sports activities.

Rahm and Cahill started officially dating in December 2015. After that, they lived together for two years before Rahm popped the question in 2018. 

They were on a hiking trip near Torrey Pines, and Rahm proposed with a custom diamond ring. 

The pair shared their vows in the beautiful city of Bilbao, Spain, in December 2019. The wedding was held in Basilica de Begoña since it was a special place for Jon and his family. 

On April 3, 2021, the couple announced the birth of their son, Kepa Cahill Rahm, on their Instagram page. 

Rahm credits his wife and son for helping him realize that there was more to life than just golf. He even went to a mental coach to work on his anger issues, and now he is more focused on being a better man for his family.

Jon Rahm Bio: FAQs

Can Jon Rahm speak English? 

English is not Rahm’s first language, but he can certainly speak and understand it well now. However, he did not speak English in Spain, so adjusting to life in the United States wasn’t easy. 

During his first semester at Arizona State University, he secured a 3.6 GPA, so it is safe to assume that he is not facing much trouble due to the language barrier. Rahm also taught himself slang words with some help from American hip-hop music. 

Has Jon Rahm won the Masters? 

As of 2021, Jon Rahm has not won a Masters Tournament. In 2018, he secured the 4th position in the Masters, but he has yet to bag the trophy.

Out of the four major championships in golf, Rahm won his first US Open in June 2021. He honored the late Spanish golfer and former world no. 1 player Seve Ballesteros after achieving the feat. 

Did Jon Rahm get COVID-19? 

Jon Rahm tested positive for COVID-19 twice in 2021. It affected his PGA Tour since he had to withdraw from the Memorial Tournament the first time it happened. 

Similarly, Rahm could not participate in the 2020 Summer Olympics after his third COVID-19 test came out positive. He was ready to leave the United States for the event in Tokyo, Japan, so it was unfortunate that he had to withdraw at the last minute.

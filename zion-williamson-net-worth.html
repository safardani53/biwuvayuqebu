Short Glance On Zion Williamson 

Zion Williamson (born July 6, 2000) was a consensus five-star recruit and was ranked among the top five players in the 2018 class. 

Besides being a basketball player, Zion also used to be a sprinter. Later, he commenced teaching as a middle school health and physical education teacher.

In 2021, William suffered a Jones fracture in his right foot and underwent subsequent surgery during the off-season.

In late September, he was expected to return for the season, but he could not return as his wound took time to heal. In mid-March of 2022, it was announced that Williamson would not play in the 2021-2022 season.

He returned for the 2022-2023 season on October 4, 2022, and made 13 points, four rebounds, one assist, and one steal in a 129-125 victory over the Chicago Bulls.

Since then, he has been performing well for his team; on 12 December, he was named the NBA Western Conference Player of the Week for Week 8, his first NBA Player of the Week award. 

He took New Orleans to an undefeated 3-0 week with an average of 33.0 points and 8.3 rebounds on 70.4% shooting from the field.

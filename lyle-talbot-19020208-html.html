Lyle Talbot is an American actor on stage and on screen, famous for his film career from 1931 to 1960 and appearing on television in the 1950s and 1960s. Ozzie Nelson, Joe Randolph, for ten years on the ABC sitcom The Adventures of Ozzie and Harriet. He began his film career under a contract with Warner Bros. in the early days of sound cinema. He has appeared in more than 150 films, first as a young matinee idol, star of many B-list films, and then as a character actor. He is a founding member of the Screen Actors Association and later serves on its board of directors. Talbot's long acting career is recounted in a book by his youngest daughter, New York-based writer Margaret Talbot, titled The Entertainer: Movies, Magic and My Father's Twent 20th Century.

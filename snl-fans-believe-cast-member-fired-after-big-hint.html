SNL fans believe cast member was FIRED after he drops big 'hint' on social media

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Wednesday, May 1, 2024

SATURDAY Night Live fans now believe that a cast member was fired after he dropped a major hint on social media.

Now in its 48th season, the long-running late-night sketch comedy show has been rumored to come to an end after its 50th.

Aristotle Ahari, 31, who joined the SNL cast in season 47, shared a cryptic message on Twitter Tuesday.

The tweet read: "Need a new hobby, looking for suggestions. Asking for a friend."

The enigmatic message alerted fans of the sketch show newcomer who quickly responded with their own concerned tweets.

One person wrote: "this is a scary tweet," to which the featured player jokingly responded, "all my tweets are scary."

Another fan commented, "I also am afraid [of] what this could mean."

Aristotle, remaining evasive, simply urged his fan to "Chill y’all, just chill."

SNL fans on Reddit expressed a similar concern for Aristotle's job status.

One commented, "I'll be pretty bummed if he gets let go after a season."

While another replied, in response to the firing speculation: "I hope not. He’s my favorite cast member. That robot bit had me wheezing. I love the singer character, too."

MOVING ON

Aristotle's tweet comes after a mass exodus of series regulars following the Saturday variety show's 47th season.

A total of four members, including the ever-popular Pete Davidson, deeply talented Kate McKinnon, show favorite Aidy Bryant, and the always quirky and aloof Kyle Mooney will not return for the show's 48th season.

Pete, 28, joined the cast in 2014 and became the first person born in the 1990s to join the SNL lineup.

During his last segment on SNL's Weekend Update, he stated: "I never imagined this would be my life. Back then, I was just a skinny kid that no one knew which race I was."

He continued: "And now everyone knows I'm white because I became hugely successful while barely showing up to work. Look at me now, I'm aging like an old banana."

Kate, 38, joined SNL for its 38th season in April of 2012.

She won Emmys, in both 2016 and 2017, for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Comedy Series for her work on the show.

During her last performance as the recurring character, Mrs. Rafferty, she said, "Earth, I love you, thanks for letting me stay awhile."

Kate then proceeded to deliver her last tagline on the show following a successful 10-year run, yelling "Live from New York it’s Saturday night."

Aidy, 35, joined SNL along with Kate on Season 38.

At the end of one of the segments, Michael Che and Bowen Yang presented Aidy with a bouquet of roses and gave her a kiss goodbye on the cheek. 

Kyle, 37, who joined the popular show back in 2013 has supplemented his SNL tenure by co-starring as Rory on the HBO comedy series Hello Ladies and starring in a 2017 film he co-wrote, Brigsby Bear.

COMING TO AN END

Speaking on Charlamagne tha God’s Hell of a Week podcast, SNL star Kenan Thompson gave a subtle hint that the famous comedy sketch series could be coming to an end within three years.

Kenan addressed rumors that SNL could end after its 50th season because creator Lorne Michaels wants to retire.

In the interview, Charlamagne asked Kenan: “The rumor is the show is ending at its 50th season because Mr. Lorne Michaels doesn’t want to do it after age 80. Is that true?”

Kenan jokingly responded by saying: “Is that the rumor? All right, I need to start planning.”

He continued: “There could be a lot of validity to that rumor because 50 is a good number to stop at. That’s an incredible package. [Michaels] will be, probably, close to 80 years old at that point, and, you know, he’s the one who’s had his touch on the whole thing."

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Monday, September 2, 2024

THE View may be a daytime talk show, but it has had plenty of moments that would make even late-night viewers blush.

From the salty language of the female hosts, to explosive fights and teases of nudity, the show has had its share of "Not Safe For Work" highlights and lowlights.

The longtime ABC talk show, first started by broadcast legend Barbara Walters, just wrapped up its 25th season.

Over the years, there have been several women hosting the popular daytime talk show.

The current lineup consists of Whoopi Goldberg, Joy Behar, Sunny Hostin, and Sara Haines.

In August the show added Ana Navarro and Alyssa Farah Griffin to the panel heading into the new season.

The show is expected back sometime in September.

It could be hard for the new hosts to follow-up some of these legendary NSFW moments.

WHOOPI'S CUSHION

Whoopi Goldberg left her co-hosts in shock earlier this month, after showing off her "rear view" during a live show.

The moderator flaunted her figure during a game with guest Michael Strahan.

The Good Morning America star joined the co-hosts to play a special The View edition of The $100,000 Pyramid, which he hosts weekly.

The first category on the board was called "Rear View."

When Michael read off the name, the 66-year-old moved her hands to her butt and jokingly covered it up.

The rest of the co-hosts laughed and Michael said: "You don't have to cover up your rearview, Whoopi. It's all good."

About five minutes into the game, Sunny and Alyssa picked the category "Rear View."

As Whoopi walked over to deliver the card, she turned around and showed her backside to the cameras, shocking her co-hosts.

Joy also came out from behind her stand and turned around to give Whoopi a glimpse of her "rear view."

Whoopi flaunted her figure and almost bowed after handing Alyssa the card.

Joy leaned over her podium and said: "Whoopi giving us a rear view."

JOY'S DELIGHT

Viewers' jaws were on the floor during the same episode, when Joy Behar made a NSFW comment while playing the game.

Another one of the categories Michael read was titled "Afternoon Delight."

That led to Joy instantly joking to Whoopi "That's what she wants," referencing the NSFW alternate meaning behind afternoon delight.

The embarrassed guest then appeared to snap at Joy over her earlier comments.

"Joy, this is a family game," he said, half snippy but seemingly half joking also.

SHERRI'S SLIP-UP

Former co-host Sherri Shepherd returned for a guest stint earlier this year, and picked up right where she left off: shocking fans after making NSFW comments.

During the live broadcast, Sherri somehow found herself revealing that her 17-year-old son Jeffrey is at the age where he’s just in his “room for hours at a time.”

Host Joy replied sarcastically: “Gee, I wonder what he’s doing in there.”

Sherri then added: “Imma tell you this, all the men in my life said just get lotion and put it in his room and back out the door.”

While some of the hosts were laughing, Ana Navarro blasted her, saying: “Sherri, TMI!”

The ex-host didn’t stop there, though, and instead turned the NSFW comments on herself.

After revealing she’s been “celibate for four years,” The 55-year-old admitted she spends a lot of alone time in her room, as well.

“I gotta lock my door ‘cause– ya know, Kandi Burruss, I order toys from Kandi Burruss all the time. And the problem is, as you get older, you get more tired.

Sherri explained that she once fell asleep with one of her "toys" still in her hand.

Her son, Jeffrey, walked in and woke her up, to both of their shocks.

"So, I’m laying and I go, ‘What are you doing?!’" she continued.

While sitting in her chair on the show, she mimicked her body jolting all over.

“The thing was still on."

Ana had had enough at that point and said it was time to move on because it was “taking a nasty turn.”

TOO FAR?

It's not always fun and games on the show.

In July, Whoopi dropped an NSFW word on live TV, and even apologized to her producers on the set.

Amid a political discussion with her co-hosts, the TV personality mediated the topic by offering a call of action to viewers.

The comedian pleaded with fans to "get out and vote" to change the representatives who have been causing controversy in office.

"All this other stuff, all the stuff that's creeping in is frighteningly dangerous to all of us, left, and right," she began.

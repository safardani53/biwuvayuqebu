What time does Sonic stop serving breakfast?

Posted by Martina Birk on Friday, June 21, 2024

SONIC is a popular drive-in fast-food restaurant known for its burgers, hotdogs, and sandwiches.

In the early 1980s, the fast-food chain started serving breakfast, and now consumers want to know what time they stop.

What time does Sonic stop serving breakfast?

While Sonic's hours of operation may vary based on location, most of them open at 6am and close at 12am, during the week.

On weekends, the chain stays open an hour longer for all your late-night hunger needs.

While most restaurants stop serving breakfast at a certain time, Sonic is among the few that offer breakfast options all throughout the day.

Who founded Sonic?

Sonic was founded in Oklahoma in 1953 and has since grown into one of the most popular fast-food chains around.

The company was founded by Troy Nuel Smith, Sr, an American businessman who got the idea of the drive-thru business while driving along the Texas-Louisiana border after his military service, according to The New York Times.

“He came up with a formula that restaurants are a people business,” said Bob Blackburn, the executive director of the Oklahoma Historical Society and the author of Sonic: The History of America’s Drive-in.

“He defined what it means to be an entrepreneur.”

Smith was born in Oklahoma on May 26, 1922, and later died on October 26, 2009, at the age of 87.

Since launching in 1953, Sonic has opened more than 3,500 locations nationwide. The company is now owned by Inspire Brands, which also owns Arby's, Buffalo Wild Wings, Jimmy John's, Rusty Taco, Mister Donut, Dunkin' Donuts, and Baskin-Robbins.

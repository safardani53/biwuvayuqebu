People are only just realising what the white lines on roads are and it's blowing their minds

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Saturday, September 21, 2024

HAVE you ever noticed the white lines in the middle of roads and wondered what they're there for? Well, you're not alone.

One confused Reddit user shared a snap of the white lines and asked what they were for, and people had some pretty wild explanations.

One user posted a picture of the white lines to the forum, Casual UK, and asked: "Anyone know what these lines mean?"

The simple question sparked a lively debate in the comments section of the post.

The lines are often seen next to speeding cameras, and there's a very good reason for it.

According to Speed Cameras UK, it is a legal requirement to have two measures of speed on the roads.

They explained: "It is a legal requirement to have a secondary measurement for speed.

"This is why at every Gatso speed camera location there are white lines painted on the road.

"The distance between each line represents between 5mph so there can be no dispute over how fast you were driving."

People flocked to the comments section of the post to share their take, and some came up with some pretty obscure reasons while others confirmed it was for speed measurements.

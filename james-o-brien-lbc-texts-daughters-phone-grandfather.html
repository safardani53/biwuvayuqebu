Who is James O'Brien? LBC radio host who discovered his daughter's texts to dead grandfather on his

Posted by Martina Birk on Monday, February 19, 2024

JAMES O'Brien is the LBC radio host who recently revealed his daughter sends secret texts to her dead granddad telling him what she’s been up to.

But, what do we know about the broadcaster? Let's get the lowdown.

James O'Brien was born on 13 January 1972- that makes him 46.

He's a journalist, television and radio presenter.

James' phone-in show on LBC airs between 10 am and 1 pm. It covers current affairs and news, views and real-life experiences.

James is a frequent critic of Brexit and the presidency of Donald Trump and has occasionally presented Newsnight for the BBC.

James is married to wife Lucy and the couple have two daughters together.

What happened when James discovered his daughter's texts to dead grandfather on his old mobile?

James recently revealed his youngest daughter texts her dead granddad.

In the sweet texts, she updates his late father, who passed away five years ago, on her life.

"I love you granddad," she says in one. "Have a great time in heaven I hope you meet Jesus.

She goes on: "I hope you had an amazing life I absolutely love you and my life could not of been this amazing without you!"

In another, she tells her grandfather that she is nearly ten and that her sister Elizabeth is 12.

The girl says her sister loved the present he got her, adding: "Your present was your love."

O'Brien was, unsurprisingly, moved by his daughter's actions when he found out.

"I may have something in my eye," he told his 291,000 Twitter followers.

James explained that his daughter uses the phone, which is disconnected from the internet, to play games.

O'Brien adds: "Still has my old contacts though & it turns out she’s been messaging my dad, who died 5 years ago."

His followers were equally moved by the tweet, with one saying: "This got me choked up and I'm from Yorkshire."

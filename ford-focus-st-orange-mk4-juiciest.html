Its the third successive Ford Focus ST in orange and this Mk4 is probably the juiciest of the

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Thursday, March 14, 2024

THEY do like orange at Ford.

I say that because this is the third successive Focus ST to be revealed looking brighter than a bag of Outspan.

We had the Electric Orange Mk2 in 2005, Tangerine Scream Mk3 in 2011 and this Mk4 launch colour is called Orange Fury.

Why? I have absolutely no idea.

But what I do know is that this latest ST is probably the best  (or juiciest) of the lot – and that includes the five-cylinder Mk2. Fast Ford fans will be fizzing like a dropped can of Tango.

OK, enough. Let’s start with the monster engine. The 2.3-litre EcoBoost is  a development of the RS engine – but the ST has faster  in-gear acceleration than an RS because it is lighter and has almost as much torque.

The numbers are 280hp and 420Nm. Which is 30 horses more than the outgoing ST and also cuts the 0-62mph sprint by eight tenths to 5.7seconds.

It’s also very sticky. The new electronic limited-slip differential improves front-end traction and the damping in Normal mode is fabulous. I really liked it.

The Recaro sports seats are impressive too: great to look at, comfortable and well positioned.

But switch to Sport or Track mode and then it all goes a bit Pete Tong.

MOST READ IN MOTORS

The ride is too firm and the steering is too springy and self-centring.

If only it had an “Individual” mode where you could select Normal steering, Normal damping and maximum attack for everything else, then it would be brilliant. But it doesn’t. The Hyundai i30N does.

If you want rev-matching and all the best noises you have to live with the skippy ride.

I have another grumble. Price. Thirty two thousand English pounds is a lot of money. If it was £28k with customisable drive settings, I’d have one tomorrow.

But otherwise I’d suggest you save £6k and have a Fiesta ST Ford Performance – in Deep Orange, obviously.

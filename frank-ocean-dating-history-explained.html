Frank Ocean's dating history explained

Posted by Martina Birk on Sunday, March 31, 2024

SINGER Frank Ocean stopped by Coachella on April 16, 2023, for his first public performance in six years.

Fans of the 33-year-old elusive songwriter want to know more about his relationship history, so here's what we know.

Who has Frank Ocean dated?

Frank Ocean tends to keep his personal life private, however, the Thinkin' Bout You singer has been mysteriously linked to a few individuals over the years.

Memo Guzman

In a 2019 interview with Gayletter, Ocean revealed he had been in a long-term relationship for quite some time.

“I don’t use dating apps. I’ve been in a relationship for three years. I definitely wasn’t using dating apps before then. I don’t think I would use dating apps now,” he told the publication for its 10th issue cover story.

“I f–k with Marc Jacobs’ philosophy on that, so I wouldn’t rule it out, but it is a little hectic being a famous person on dating apps.”

Fans believe the man Ocean was referring to back then was Memo Guzman since they had been spotted together in the past and Ocean even mentioned him in his song, Provider.

The speculations, however, have never been confirmed by either party.

Omar Apollo

Rumor also has it that Ocean has been previously linked to singer, Omar Apollo, 25.

One fan, @oliviaaspina, took to TikTok to post a video explaining her theory that the pair had spent a vacation in Italy with friends in August 2022.

However, again, Ocean has yet to confirm or deny any of these rumors, nor his true relationship status.

What happened to Frank Ocean at Coachella 2023?

Frank Ocean’s headlining set at Coachella 2023 marked his first performance in six years.

On Sunday, April 16, 2023, Ocean started his performance nearly an hour late but was forced to end his set just after midnight.

Ocean said abruptly after a song: “Guys, I’m being told it’s curfew, so that’s the end of the show. Thank you so much.”

The disjointed ending left many fans in disbelief, especially given the set was such a long time coming.

His set was also pulled from live-streaming, with YouTube confirming the news on Twitter, writing: “Frank Ocean is not scheduled to appear on the Coachella live stream.”

Ocean was originally due to perform at Coachella in 2020 before the festival was canceled due to the pandemic.

He last performed at the Flow Festival in 2017 in Finland.

Although Ocean debuted two singles in 2020, the singer hasn't released a new studio album since 2016, when Blonde was shared with the world.

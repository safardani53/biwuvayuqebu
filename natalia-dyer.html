Natalia Dyer Career

Early in her adolescence, Dyer participated in projects made locally and close to her native state of Tennessee.

In 2009, she played the character of Clarissa Granger in Hannah Montana: The Movie.

Prior to acting in the modest independent films Don’t Let Me Go and After Darkness, both of which were released the following years, she had small roles in the movies The Greening of Whitney Brown and Blue Like Jazz at this time.

Her first significant role came when she was sixteen and she filmed the independent film I Believe in Unicorns. At the 2014 SXSW Film Festival, the movie had its world premiere after three years.

As Nancy Wheeler in the Netflix series Stranger Things, Dyer had her breakout performance.

Additionally, Dyer has acted in a number of independent movies, most notably the dramedy Yes, God, yes (2019), in which she plays Alice, a Catholic schoolgirl who is experimenting with her sexuality.

She has a number of prominent credits, including supporting parts in the 2019 horror movies Velvet Buzzsaw and Things Heard & Seen (2021).

She will co-star with Asa Butterfield in the horror movie All Fun and Games, which will be released in January 2022.

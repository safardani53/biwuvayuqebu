Nikita Kucherov | Short Description

Nikita Kucherov is a professional Russian ice hockey player who serves as a right-winger for the Tampa Bay Lightning of the NHL.

Tampa Bay drafted Nikita 58th overall in the 2011 NHL Entry Draft. Since then, Nikita has served nine years with the team and recently continued his contract for more than five years.

Not to be underlooked, Nikita is also considered one of the NHL’s top offensive performers.

In addition to that, he won the Art Ross Trophy as the league’s leading scorer and the Hart Memorial Trophy as the MVP. Not to forget, also the Ted Lindsay Award as the best player voted by the NHL players.

Nikita Kucherov | Bio, Family & Early Hockey Career

The professional ice hockey player for the Tampa Bay Lightning, Kucherov, was born in Maykop, Russia, on June 17, 1993. Despite being an American League player, Nikita’s nationality is Russian, while his ethnicity happens to be white.

As for his family, Kucherov was born to his parents, Igor Kucherov and Svetland Kucherov.

Talking about Nikita’s hockey life, he professionally played for CSKA Moscow, the Kontinental Hockey League(KHL).

After that, in 2011, the Tampa Bay Lightning selected the Russian player 58th overall in the 2011 NHL Entry Draft.

Then, Kucherov sported significant junior hockey with the Rouyn-Noranda Huskies of the Quebec Major Junior Hockey League(QMJHL). Subsequently, on September 10, 2012, he signed a three-year entry-level contract with Lightning.

The juvenile right-winger joined Martin St. Louis, on May 20, 2015, as the only player in Tampa history to record two game-winning goals in overtime.

Succeedingly, on October 11, 2016, Tampa Bay renewed the contract with the young athlete to a three-year deal worth $4.766 million per season.

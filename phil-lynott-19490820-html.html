Philip Parris Lynott is an Irish singer, songwriter and musician. His most commercially successful band is Thin Lizzy, of which he is the founding member, lead musician, lead vocalist and bassist. He is known for his imaginative lyrical contributions, including stories of the working class and many characters drawn from his personal influences and Celtic culture. Lynott was born in the West Midlands of England, but raised in Dublin with his grandparents. He remained close to his mother, Philomena, throughout his life. He led several groups as main vocalists, including Skid Row along with Gary Moore, before learning bass guitar and founding Thin Lizzy in 1969. Following early success with "Whiskey in the Jar ", the group enjoyed a number of hits in the mid-1970s like" The Boys Are Back In Town "," Jailbreak "and" Waiting for an Alibi ", and became a popular live attraction that incorporated vocal and composition features of Lynott with a pair of main guitars.

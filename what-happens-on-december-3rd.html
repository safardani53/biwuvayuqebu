What Happens on Dec. 3? You Might Want to Grab a Sweater

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Thursday, March 21, 2024

People on social media have been buzzing about what happens on Dec. 3. Here’s what we know — and why you might want to be sure to bring a sweater.

There are some dates that just stick in everyone’s mind forever and ever. If Oct. 3 comes around and you don’t say “It’s October third” exactly like Cady in Mean Girls, then you are simply not living life to the fullest. The same goes for observing April 25 as a perfect date “because it’s not too hot and not too cold.”

And now, you may have noticed that everyone is talking about Dec. 3 as being particularly momentous, especially if you’re active on TikTok. But why is that? What happens on Dec. 3? Here’s what we know.

Article continues below advertisement

What happens on Dec. 3?

Depending on how plugged-in you are to TikTok trends, there’s a good chance you may already know that the song “Heather” by Conan Gray has been seriously huge on the video-sharing app for a while. It may have just started as a good song that a bunch of people liked, but at this point, it has reached new levels of popularity and even jumpstarted a couple of trends.

For instance, you may remember when people started doing the Pinterest trend, where they hid a video of themselves standing stock-still among a bunch of Pinterest search results and only revealed the secret when a certain part of the song “Heather” played in the video. From there, people started referring to other people who are lovely and lovable (but who maybe don't realize it) as "Heathers."

Article continues below advertisement

The word “Heather” became a sort of shorthand on the app, and now the song is striking again! If you’re only familiar with the snippet of the song that has become popular on TikTok, then you might not know that the lyrics start with the line, “I still remember, third of December.” That’s right, people. The third of December! Dec. 3!

Article continues below advertisement

The song continues, “Me in your sweater, you said it looked better / On me, than it did you, only if you knew / How much I liked you.” So, people are talking about Dec. 3 because it’s specifically referenced in “Heather” and just happens to be the date on which the narrator in the song is pining for their crush (who only had eyes for Heather).

Article continues below advertisement

To mark the occasion of Dec. 3, some people plan to give their sweater to their crush or even ask them out. Others just plan to wear a sweater and maybe cry a few cathartic tears while listening to “Heather” on repeat. Some even hope to receive a sweater from someone else themselves.

If someone hands you a sweater on Dec. 3, it may indicate that they have feelings for you (but don’t jump to conclusions too quickly — maybe you just look chilly!). And if you’re the one with the crush, maybe you’ll want to give them your sweater! Even if you don’t have a crush, though, it’s never a bad idea to keep an extra sweater handy in December.

Posted by Martina Birk on Friday, April 26, 2024

CLEANING the oven is one of the most hated kitchen tasks so we’ve rounded up the best oven liners to make things a little easier.

These handy sheets go on the bottom shelf of your appliance and protect it from drips and spills - all you need to do is wipe it clean after each use.

The idea is that you place these on the bottom shelf of your oven to catch any drips or spills, whether you’re heating up a pizza or slow cooking a stew.

The silicone is non-stick, which means that - unlike when food drops to the bottom of your oven and becomes a burnt-on sticky mess - anything that falls on the oven liner is easy to wash off, drastically reducing the time needed to clean your oven.

It’s important that you place it on the bottom shelf of your oven rather than directly on the base as otherwise it can overheat and even start a fire (see below).

Can oven liners be used in gas ovens?

Oven liners can be used safely in gas ovens but they must be placed on the bottom shelf of the oven rather than directly over the base.

This is to avoid it touching the open flame - the liners are not designed to be used over direct heat - or cover any holes on the base that could cause a buildup of toxic carbon monoxide.

Even if you’re using it in an electric oven, it’s still advisable to have the liner on the bottom shelf.

This is because some ovens also have heating elements in the base of the appliance, which could cause the liner to overheat, melt or even catch fire.

Do oven liners affect temperature?

Any item in the oven can affect oven temperature - an oven liner included.

In the case of an oven liner, it can affect the circulation of hot air, meaning the heat might not be distributed evenly.

This can be a problem if you’re baking bread or cakes, where precise and even temperatures are important, but less so if you’re making a stew.

The effect can also vary depending on the type of liner and the type of oven you have.

Trimming back the liner so it just fits on the bottom shelf - and doesn’t hang over the edge - can help. But if you need controlled temperature for baking bread or cakes, it’s best to take out the liner.

Are silicone oven liners safe?

Silicone oven liners should be made from food-grade materials that won’t break down when they’re heated up, which means they’re considered non-toxic.

That said, you need to follow the instructions for your liner.

Most are oven safe up to 260C and can be put into the dishwasher but this can vary so always double check.

It’s also important to use it correctly to prevent it from becoming a fire hazard.

Are copper oven liners safe to use?

Like silicone oven liners, copper oven liners should be safe if used correctly.

This means putting it on the bottom shelf rather than the base of your oven, trimming it to size and making sure it’s not overheated.

While handy for keeping your oven free from drips and spills, oven liners should still be cleaned and rinsed regularly, ideally after every use.

Early Life

Brian Francisco Flores, famously known as Brian Flores, was born on February 24, 1981. He was born in Brooklyn, New York.

The former Dolphins coach grew up and spent much of his childhood in the Brownsville section of Brooklyn. Flores was born to Raul Flores and Maria Flores, both Hondurans.

The 6’1″ coach attended the Poly Prep Country Day School in Brooklyn, New York. In 1999, he played football for Boston College as a linebacker. He continued to play football for the same until 2002. 

He was ready to be a part of the NFL, but he soon picked up an injury that prevented him from entering the NFL at the time. 

Flores has repeatedly stated that he grew up as a fan of the New York Mets and the New York Giants. 

Flores has stated that he almost became a victim of Police violence during his teenage years in Brooklyn.

Brian Flores: Coaching Career

Let’s glance at each of the teams in which Brian Flores has been active as a coach. 

New England Patriots

A highly regarded NFL coach, Flores started his NFL career with the New England Patriots. 

Although he became a part of their coaching staff in 2008, he was already in the Patriots’ setup from early 2004.

In 2004, he joined the team as a scouting assistant. And by 2006, he was a pro scout. He later got promoted to the coaching staff in 2008.

He served the team for a long time, from 2008 to 2018, in various coaching roles. In 2008, he was active as a special teams assistant. In the upcoming years, he served in the following positions at the Patriots:

  • Assistant Offense & Special Teams Coach [2010]
  • Defensive Assistant [2011]
  • Safeties Coach [2012-2015]
  • Linebackers Coach [2016-2018]

Flores was the safety coach for a 4-year tenure. And in his stint as a safeties coach, the New England Patriots defeated the Seattle Seahawks to win their 4th Super Bowl title in Super Bowl XLIX. 

During Flores’ final season at the New England Patriots, they won the Super Bowl title in Super Bowl LIII. 

After the victory with the Patriots, he became a part of the Miami Dolphins. He served as the head coach there from 2019 to 2021. 

Miami Dolphins

In 2019, just the next day after the Patriots won the Super Bowl title, Flores was hired by the Miami Dolphins. With this, he became the 12th head coach of the Miami Dolphins and the 4th Latino head coach in the NFL. 

In Flores’ coaching debut at the Miami Dolphins, the team lost to the Baltimore Ravens in 2019. The losing streaks continued for some time for the coach.

In week two, the Dolphins lost to Flores’ former team, the New England Patriots. The worst part was that the Dolphins had blowout losses consistently. 

Flores’ coaching stint at the Miami Dolphins was winless for two months. He eventually got into winning ways. His first season at the Miami Dolphins ended with a 27-24 win over the Patriots. The win was Miami Dolphins’ only road win since 2008. 

2020 and Beyond

Brian’s second season with the Miami Dolphins was no different than his first one. His second season, too, started with back-to-back losses. 

The Dolphins’ first win of the season was against the Jacksonville Jaguars. And this was also Miami’s only win over the Jaguars since 2014. 

His coaching career at the Miami Dolphins was always hyped about the wins over his former team, the New England Patriots.

In 2020 too, the Miami Dolphins defeated the New England Patriots, eliminating the Patriots from the playoffs. This season was the first winning season of the Dolphins under Flores’ tenure and the first one since 2016. 

In 2021, Flores joined an elite list of head coaches in the Miami Dolphins’ history after the opening win to beat the Patriots multiple times in Foxboro. 

The American coach had an interesting season with the Miami Dolphins in 2021. They concluded their season with a win against the New England Patriots. 

Being a wonderful coach, Flores could change the fortunes of the Miami Dolphins. The Miami Dolphins lost seven consecutive games and later returned to win seven straight games.

The Miami Dolphins, thus, became the first team in the NFL to lose 7 consecutive games and win seven straight games in the same season. 

Under Brian Flores’ stint, the Miami Dolphins had back-to-back winning seasons. And this was the only instance since the 2002 & 2003 seasons. 

In January 2022, unfortunately, he was fired by the Miami Dolphins. Flores had two years more in his contract with the Miami Dolphins. The move was a surprise to everyone in the NFL.

Wendys to make major change to fan favorite and customers will be happy as it costs just one

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Saturday, April 27, 2024

WENDY’S is introducing a major menu change that will bring the cost of a fan-favorite down to just one cent.

The fast-food chain is celebrating National Hamburger Month with some mouth-watering deals.

Wendy’s is offering its junior bacon cheeseburger for just one cent with any other purchase. 

The limited-time deal is valid from May 26 to June 1.

There’s no minimum order required but customers must order via the Wendy’s app, and the offer can only be used once.

When ordering alone, a junior bacon cheeseburger is $3.19, although prices can vary by location.

The offer comes as part of National Hamburger Month and National Hamburger Day (May 28) celebrations.

Wendy’s has a whole host of other deals through May including a buy one, get one for $1 on burgers, all month long.

Meanwhile, customers can score a free crispy chicken sandwich or crispy chicken BLT with a $10 in-app purchase from May 15 to May 21.

Another freebie is up for grabs, as customers can get a select free soft drink with any purchase that is ordered via the Wendy’s app from May 29 to June 4.

Most read in Money

Aside from these deals, Wendy’s has also brought back its $3 breakfast combo, which gives you $6 worth of products for half the price.

Included in the bag is a bacon or sausage and egg Swiss croissant and a small side of seasoned potatoes.

The deal can be redeemed either on the app or in-store.

However, you might not want to wait too long, as the breakfast promotion will only last until June 4.

IN THE CAN

But what if you don’t have a Wendy’s near you?

The chain announced last month that it is bringing its famous chili to grocery stores.

Wendy’s chefs have teamed up with Conagra Brands to serve a beef chili with 29 grams of protein in a 15oz can for $4.99. 

MORE FAST FOOD NEWS

Meanwhile, Wendy’s competitor McDonald’s has announced it is making some subtle improvements to enhance its signature hamburgers.

Expect softer, brioche-style buns, and meltier cheese.

Onions will now be cooked with the burgers on the grill, giving the patties a more caramelized flavor.

Plus, customers can expect to taste more of that beloved Big Mac sauce in every bite.

The changes will be making their way throughout the US this year, and some stores on the West Coast are already rolling them out.

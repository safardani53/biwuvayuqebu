Jeopardy! fans roast contestants for botching 'easy' category about legendary rock band in painfu

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Wednesday, May 15, 2024

A JEOPARDY! category about The Beatles went so poorly that the internet twisted and shouted online.

On Monday's episode, only two of the five well-known songs by the beloved band dawned on the contestants in total.

The contestants were Année Tousseau, eventual winner Dillon Hupp and returning two-day champ Emma Hill Kepron.

Ken Jennings, 48, presented "Beatles 'S'ongs" by explaining each clue was looking for a song by The Beatles that ends in "S."

The $200 clue read: "So may I introduce to you the act you've known for all these years?"

Dillon - who revealed he won his statewide quiz bowl competition in Arkansas and emerged with $22K total- in correctly got it as Sgt. Peppers Lonely Heart's Club Band.

However from there on out, the Jeopardy! round about the Fab Four's discography- the best-selling by any artist of all time- didn't go so fabulously.

The $400 clue read: "Nothing is real and nothing to get hung about"

Everyone just stared, Ken told them after the time ran out: "In 'Strawberry Fields Forever.'"

The $600 clue read: "With a love like that, you know you should be glad"

Most read in Entertainment

Emma buzzed in but blanked and no one else responded. Ken remarked her rival seemed like he was getting there mentally: "Oh, Dillon just got the chorus - 'She Loves You.'"

The $800 clue read: "I don't want to leave her now, you know I believe and how."

Dillon replied: What is Something in the way she moves?"

Ken, after a slight hesitation, deemed his response correct despite that not being the correct title.

He ruled: "Yes, by George Harrison. 'Something.'"

Finally, the $1000 clue read: "Sunday's on the phone to Monday, Tuesday's on the phone to me"

After another lengthy silence, Ken said: "Off Abbey Road. 'She Came In Through The Bathroom Window.'"

Fans of the game show and music lovers came together to roast the rough round on Twitter.

'SCREAMING AND SHAKING MY HEAD'

One user wrote: "How is it possible that no one knows any Beatles songs?"

Another wrote: "I can’t root for any of these contestants after their poor showing in Beatles songs that begin with 'S.'"

And a third wrote: "Watching them miss those Beatles clues was painful!!!"

A fourth was bothered by Ken's breezy ruling: "Uhhhhh…'Something In The Way She Moves,” despite being correct as a lyric, is a James Taylor song.  The needful BEATLES title is simply 'Something.'"

Another said they were "Just screaming and shaking my head," as a sixth wrote: "My dad was about to flip out."

A sixth lamented: "Nobody cares for the Beatles anymore. Especially younger folks lol."

Contestant Emma wrote about the Strawberry Fields miss on Reddit: "'Lucy in the Sky with Diamonds' got in my brain first. I did, for a nanosecond, consider ringing in and hoping for a miracle, but I probably would have said "Slucy in the Sky with Diamonds."

THE ANSWER IS BLOWING IN THE WIND

In February, Ken looked baffled by the incorrect guesses for a Bob Dylan song title.

The $400 clue under the category “Bob Dylan Lyrics" read: "Hey 'this guy,' Play A Song For Me, I’m Not Sleepy, And There Is No Place I’m Going To.'"

None of the contestants could guess "Mr. Tamborine Man" but had some out-of-tune alternatives.

2-day winner Mira Hayward guessed: “Who is Piano Man,” seemingly confusing Bob Dylan with Billy Joel.

The next contestant, Kendra Westerhaus, guessed: “Who is Joe?”

Ken broke the awkward silence by comforting: "We had some Jimi Hendrix Billy Joel confusion there."

"PAIN," one user wrote sharing the woeful clip. "Imagine going through life thinking Piano Man was a Dylan song, sad," mourned another.
